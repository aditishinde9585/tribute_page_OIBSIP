About Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, affectionately known as the "Missile Man of India," was born on 15 October 1931 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu. He was a renowned aerospace scientist who played a crucial role in India's civilian space program and military missile development efforts.

He significantly contributed to India's success in satellite launch vehicles, ballistic missiles, and nuclear capabilities. His leadership in the Pokhran-II tests and work at ISRO and DRDO earned him the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award.

Dr. Kalam served as the 11th President of India (2002‚Äì2007), and was widely respected for his humility, wisdom, and integrity. A lifelong advocate of youth empowerment, he remained active in education and public speaking until his passing in 2015, leaving behind a legacy of inspiration.
